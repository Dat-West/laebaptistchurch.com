Our Church Anual Timeline

Opening of Church Ministries

3rd & 4th Week of February

We are thrilled to open our Church Ministries, designed to enrich your spiritual journey and foster community. Whether you’re seeking deeper study, fellowship, or service opportunities, we have something for everyone. Join us as we embark on this exciting chapter together, united by faith and purpose.

Back to School Crusade

01st to 03rd of March

As we gather together, we celebrate the start of a new academic year—a time of fresh beginnings, renewed hope, and growing faith. Our crusade is a joyful occasion where students, parents, and educators come together to seek God’s guidance, blessings, and protection for the journey ahead.

Easter Fellowship

29th to 31st of March

Join us for inspiring worship, heartfelt messages, and fellowship with fellow believers. Together, we celebrate the victory over sin and death, as Christ emerged from the tomb, bringing eternal life to all who believes. Let’s embrace this season of grace, forgiveness, and transformation.

Mother's Day Celebration

12th of May

Mother’s Day holds immense significance in our church community. It’s a time to honor mothers and mother figures, expressing our appreciation for their love, sacrifice, and nurturing care. Beyond the commercial aspects, Mother’s Day serves as a reminder of biblical teachings on motherhood and the vital role women play in shaping future generations. Let us celebrate the divine gift of motherhood together!

Thanks Giving

01st Week of June

As a part of the Baptist Union Community in Papua New Guinea, our congregation joyfully participates in the annual Thanksgiving Offering, which directly benefits the students, teachers and staff members and the operation of the Baptist Theology College located in the district of Mul, Baiyer, Lumusa in Western Highland Province.

School Holiday Crusade - Mid Year

21st to 23rd of June

As the holiday season approaches, we gather with excitement and anticipation. Our Student Holiday Crusade is a vibrant celebration where young hearts come together to worship, learn, and connect.

National Repentence Day

26th of August

On this solemn occasion, our nation comes together in reflection and humility. It’s a day to acknowledge our shortcomings, seek forgiveness, and turn our hearts toward reconciliation and renewal. Through prayer, repentance, and acts of compassion, we strive to build a better future for all. May this day be a time of healing and unity

Father's Day Celebration

01st of September

Father’s Day holds immense significance in our church community. It provides an opportunity to honor and appreciate the important role of fathers in raising families with faith and love. Through inspiring stories, biblical readings, and reflections, we deepen our understanding of the sacred duty of fatherhood. Fathers serve as spiritual leaders, modeling righteous character and demonstrating God’s love to their families. By celebrating and supporting fathers within the church, we reinforce their essential role in the kingdom of God and inspire other men to embrace their responsibilities with joy and grace

Baptism

28th of September

In this sacred moment, we celebrate lives transformed by faith. Baptism symbolizes spiritual rebirth—a cleansing of the old and a rising into newness of life. As candidates step into the water, they declare their commitment to follow Christ. Join us as we witness this beautiful expression of faith and obedience.

AGM

10th of November

Our AGM is a significant event in the life of our church. The AGM allows us to conduct official business for the year. During this gathering, we: Review the “annual report,” celebrating what God has done in and through our church family. Elect new leaders (Vestry members) who steward our finances and advise our lead pastor on ministry matters1.

Closing of Church Ministries

3rd & 4th Week of November

As the year draws to a close, we gather to reflect, reconcile, and prepare for the year ahead. In this time, we reflect, celebrate and acknowledge the blessings of God, the Christian challenges and break throughs and we pray for God's continuous will, guidance, strength and blessings for the coming new year.

Christmas Carols by Candle Night

8th of December

Gather 'round as we embrace the warmth of candlelight and the timeless melodies of the season. Our Christmas Carols service is a cherished tradition—a moment to celebrate the birth of Christ through song, scripture, and community. Join us as we raise our voices in joyous harmony, illuminating the darkness with hope. Let the flickering flames remind us of the Light of the World who came to bring peace and salvation. From “Silent Night” to “Hark! The Herald Angels Sing,” every carol carries a message of love and redemption.

Youth BUSA or NBYF Camping

17th 22nd of December

As the calendar turns its final pages, our youth community gathers for a special celebration where we look back on the year—its challenges, victories, and growth. We’ll express gratitude for God’s faithfulness, lift our voices in praise during our worship sessions and Dive into inspiring messages that prepare us for the new year. There we Connect with fellow youth, sharing laughter, dreams, and encouragement. Together, we’re stronger. And also we step into the future, as we cast vision for our youth ministry. Join us as we bid farewell to this year and embrace the possibilities of the next.

Christmas Fellowship & Child Dedication

23rd 24th 25th December

Our Christmas Fellowship is a joyous gathering where we celebrate the birth of Jesus Christ. It’s a time for worship, recieving God's Word, and reflection. We take this time also for Child Dedication which is a beautiful moment when parents publicly commit to raising their child in a home centered on Jesus. During a church service, parents bring their child before the congregation. They express gratitude for this precious gift and solemnly promise to nurture their child’s faith. Through prayer and support from the church community, we dedicate these young lives to God’s care and guidance.
